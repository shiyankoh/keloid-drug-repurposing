## Codebase Patterns
- ChEMBL API: resolve drug name via `molecule.json?pref_name=<NAME>`, then fetch mechanisms via `mechanism.json?molecule_chembl_id=<ID>`
- ChEMBL primary targets often don't match our 22-gene list — especially for biologics and IMiDs. Fall back to PubMed for gene-specific directionality.
- Annotations JSON is at Keloid project root: `data/directionality_annotations.json`, not in the ralph subdirectory
- Research docs go in `docs/directionality/` at the Keloid project root
- TNF is context_dependent, all other target genes are pro_keloid
- Each JSON entry needs exactly 8 fields: drug_name, gene_symbol, action_direction, confidence, source, keloid_target_role, net_effect, notes
