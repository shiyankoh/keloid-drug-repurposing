## Summary

Lenalidomide acts primarily through cereblon-mediated ubiquitin ligase modulation but has well-documented downstream effects on all four target genes. It inhibits TNF-alpha production (a core IMiD property), suppresses VEGFA expression and VEGF receptor signaling (anti-angiogenic), and disrupts beta-catenin associations with cell adhesion molecules. In the keloid context, inhibition of pro-fibrotic/pro-angiogenic pathways is generally beneficial, though the TNF effect is marked unclear due to TNF's context-dependent role.
