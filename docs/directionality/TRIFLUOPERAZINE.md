## Summary

Trifluoperazine (TFP) is a phenothiazine antipsychotic whose keloid-relevant effects are indirect, operating primarily through calmodulin antagonism and NUPR1 inhibition rather than direct kinase or transcription factor targeting. For STAT3, the evidence is contradictory: calmodulin/CaMKII positively regulates STAT3 activation (suggesting calmodulin antagonism would reduce p-STAT3), but the only experimental paper testing TFP directly (PMID 32195026, melanoma) showed dose-dependent p-STAT3 *increases* — possibly a compensatory response. Direction is therefore unknown. For SMAD3, CaMKII mediates TGF-β/SMAD activation in fibrosis models, and TFP demonstrated anti-fibrotic effects in an osteoarthritis model via NUPR1 inhibition (PMID 39090667), though that study paradoxically showed increased SMAD3 pathway activation alongside reduced fibrosis markers. The net anti-fibrotic effect supports an inhibitor designation at low confidence.

## Sources
- ChEMBL: CHEMBL422 — molecule record, no mechanism-of-action entries
- PubMed:32195026 — TFP increases p-STAT3 in melanoma brain metastasis model; STAT3-dependent lysosomal membrane permeabilization
- PubMed:39090667 — TFP (NUPR1 inhibitor) reduces synovial fibrosis in DMM osteoarthritis mice; fibrosis reduction partially mediated by SMAD3 signaling
- PubMed:40300936 — TFP repurposing review for glioblastoma; targets calmodulin, DRD2, NUPR1
- CaM/CaMKII-STAT3: calmodulin 2 activates JAK2/STAT3/HIF-1A/VEGFA signaling in gastric cancer
- CaMKII-SMAD: CaMKII inhibition reduces SMAD2 activation and fibrosis markers in renal and hepatic fibrosis models
