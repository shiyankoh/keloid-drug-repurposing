## Summary

Peginterferon alfa-2b is a type I interferon that signals through IFNAR1/IFNAR2, activating the JAK-STAT pathway. Its canonical signaling uses JAK1 and TYK2, but JAK2 has also been shown to associate with the IFNAR complex and participate in type I IFN signaling, making it an activator of JAK2 — which is unfavorable in the pro-keloid context. IFN-α has documented anti-proliferative effects that can downregulate EGFR expression and signaling in certain cell types, though evidence in fibroblasts is limited. IFN-α generally suppresses pro-inflammatory cytokine production including IL-6 in fibrotic contexts, consistent with its clinical use as an anti-fibrotic agent in keloids. The net profile is mixed: beneficial EGFR/IL-6 suppression but potentially unfavorable JAK2 activation.
