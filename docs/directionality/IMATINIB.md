## Summary

Imatinib is a multi-kinase inhibitor targeting ABL, PDGFR-β, and c-KIT. None of our four keloid target genes are direct kinase targets of imatinib. However, imatinib has well-documented anti-fibrotic effects via c-Abl-mediated blockade of non-canonical TGF-β signaling (reducing SMAD3 phosphorylation indirectly). It does not inhibit EGFR — that is a distinct kinase family. JAK2 modulation is indirect and poorly characterized. Some evidence suggests Wnt/β-catenin pathway modulation in GIST and thyroid cancer contexts, but relevance to keloid fibroblasts is uncertain.
