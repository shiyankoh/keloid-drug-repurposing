# ASPIRIN — Directionality Research

## Drug Class
NSAID (non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug) / Cyclooxygenase inhibitor

## ChEMBL ID
CHEMBL25

## ChEMBL Mechanism
Cyclooxygenase inhibitor (target CHEMBL2094253, COX-1/COX-2). No direct matches for PIK3CA, TGFB1, or CYP24A1.

## Gene-by-Gene Directionality

| Gene | Direction | Confidence | Net Effect | Rationale |
|------|-----------|------------|------------|-----------|
| PIK3CA | inhibitor | medium | beneficial | Aspirin inhibits PI3K/AKT signaling; disrupts K-Ras–p110α interaction and is more effective in PIK3CA-mutant cells. COX-independent mechanism. |
| TGFB1 | inhibitor | medium | beneficial | Aspirin suppresses TGF-β1–Smad2/3 pathway, reducing fibrosis markers. Aspirin-triggered resolvin D1 further blocks TGF-β1-induced EMT. Direct fibrosis relevance. |
| CYP24A1 | unknown | low | unclear | No published evidence of aspirin modulating CYP24A1 expression or enzyme activity. Only association is a pharmacogenomic SNP (rs4809957) linked to aspirin-induced GI bleeding risk — not a mechanistic effect. |

## Summary

Aspirin (CHEMBL25) is a COX-1/COX-2 inhibitor whose primary mechanism (prostaglandin synthesis inhibition) does not directly target any of the three genes in our keloid panel. However, aspirin has well-documented COX-independent effects on PI3K/AKT signaling (disrupting K-Ras–p110α interaction, enhanced efficacy in PIK3CA-mutant colorectal cancer) and TGF-β1 signaling (suppressing TGF-β1–Smad2/3-mediated fibrosis in endometrial models). Both effects are beneficial against pro-keloid targets. CYP24A1 has no documented mechanistic link to aspirin — the only PubMed hit is a pharmacogenomic association unrelated to drug action.
