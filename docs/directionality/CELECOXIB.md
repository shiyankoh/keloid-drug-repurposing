# CELECOXIB — Directionality Research

## Drug Class
COX-2 selective inhibitor (NSAID)

## ChEMBL ID
CHEMBL118

## ChEMBL Mechanism
Cyclooxygenase-2 (PTGS2) inhibitor — CHEMBL230. Primary target is COX-2; effects on STAT3, PIK3CA, VEGFA, and CTNNB1 are indirect or off-target.

## Gene-by-Gene Directionality

| Gene | Direction | Confidence | Source | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|------------|--------|-----------|
| STAT3 | inhibitor | medium | PubMed:17052991, PubMed:16707460 | COX-2-independent inhibition of STAT3 phosphorylation. Celecoxib directly inhibits STAT3 activation at concentrations achievable in vivo, demonstrated in glioblastoma, HCC, and HNSCC models. |
| PIK3CA | inhibitor | medium | PubMed:15623620, PubMed:17145890 | Celecoxib suppresses PI3K/AKT signaling in multiple cancer models. Some evidence suggests COX-2-independent mechanism via direct PDK1 inhibition upstream of AKT, which feeds back on PI3K pathway activity. |
| VEGFA | inhibitor | medium | PubMed:12438237, PubMed:15956284 | COX-2 inhibition reduces PGE2-mediated VEGF induction. Celecoxib decreases VEGF expression in tumor and endothelial cells. Well-documented anti-angiogenic property of COX-2 inhibitors. |
| CTNNB1 | inhibitor | medium | PubMed:16166264, PubMed:15767574 | Celecoxib downregulates Wnt/β-catenin signaling in colorectal cancer models. Reduces nuclear β-catenin accumulation and TCF/LEF transcriptional activity, partially independent of COX-2 inhibition. |

## Summary
Celecoxib is a selective COX-2 inhibitor (CHEMBL118) whose primary anti-inflammatory mechanism is prostaglandin synthesis blockade. However, celecoxib has well-documented COX-2-independent effects on several signaling pathways relevant to keloid pathogenesis. It inhibits STAT3 phosphorylation directly, suppresses PI3K/AKT signaling (possibly via PDK1), reduces VEGFA expression through PGE2 depletion, and downregulates Wnt/β-catenin transcriptional activity. All four target genes are pro-keloid, so inhibition is beneficial.
