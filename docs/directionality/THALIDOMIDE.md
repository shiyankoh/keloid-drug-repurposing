## Summary

Thalidomide is the original IMiD, first recognized for its potent TNF-alpha inhibitory activity (Sampaio et al. 1991). It accelerates TNF-alpha mRNA degradation and was the first drug shown to have this property clinically. Its anti-angiogenic effects — including VEGF suppression — drove investigation in multiple myeloma and solid tumors. Like lenalidomide, thalidomide modulates Wnt/β-catenin signaling via cereblon-mediated substrate degradation. All three keloid-relevant gene targets show inhibitor directionality, though TNF's net effect is unclear due to its context-dependent role in keloid pathogenesis.
