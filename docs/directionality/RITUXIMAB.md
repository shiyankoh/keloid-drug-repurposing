# RITUXIMAB — Directionality Research

## Drug Class
Monoclonal antibody (anti-CD20)

## ChEMBL ID
CHEMBL1201576

## Primary Mechanism
B-lymphocyte antigen CD20 binding agent. Depletes CD20+ B cells via complement-dependent cytotoxicity (CDC), antibody-dependent cellular cytotoxicity (ADCC), and direct apoptosis induction.

## Gene-by-Gene Directionality

| Gene | Action Direction | Confidence | Source | Notes |
|------|-----------------|------------|--------|-------|
| IL6 | inhibitor | medium | PubMed:17469107, PubMed:20305122 | Indirect — B cell depletion reduces IL-6 production. RA studies show significant serum IL-6 reduction post-rituximab. |
| JAK2 | unknown | low | ChEMBL:CHEMBL1201576, PubMed review | No direct or well-documented indirect effect on JAK2. Any effect would be downstream of reduced cytokine milieu after B cell depletion. |
| TGFB1 | inhibitor | low | PubMed:21623684, PubMed:26004142 | Indirect — B cell depletion may reduce TGF-β1 in fibrotic contexts. Limited evidence from systemic sclerosis studies showing reduced TGF-β signaling after rituximab. |

## Summary
Rituximab is an anti-CD20 monoclonal antibody whose primary mechanism (B cell depletion) does not directly target IL6, JAK2, or TGFB1. Its effects on these genes are entirely indirect: B cell depletion reduces cytokine production (particularly IL-6) and may modulate TGF-β1 in fibrotic disease. JAK2 is an intracellular kinase with no clear link to rituximab's mechanism. Confidence is low-to-medium given the indirect nature of these effects.
