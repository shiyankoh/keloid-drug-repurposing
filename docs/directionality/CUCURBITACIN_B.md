# CUCURBITACIN B — Directionality Research

## Drug Class
Natural product triterpenoid; STAT3 inhibitor. Isolated from Cucurbitaceae family plants.

## ChEMBL ID

## Gene-by-Gene Directionality

| Gene | Direction | Confidence | Net Effect | Rationale |
|------|-----------|------------|------------|-----------|

| SMAD3 | inhibitor | low | beneficial | No direct CuB-SMAD3 evidence in literature. However, CuB downregulates TGF-β1 expression via SIRT1/IGFBPrP1 axis in liver fibrosis models, and TGF-β1 signals through SMAD3. Indirect inference only. |

## Summary

Cucurbitacin B is a potent, direct STAT3 inhibitor with strong preclinical evidence across cancer and fibrosis models. The STAT3 interaction has been validated by cell thermal shift assay (CETSA), confirming physical binding. CuB inhibits STAT3 phosphorylation at the post-translational level, leading to downstream anti-proliferative and anti-fibrotic effects including reduced collagen-1α and α-SMA expression. For SMAD3, there is no direct evidence — but CuB suppresses TGF-β1 via SIRT1 activation in hepatic fibrosis, which would indirectly reduce SMAD3 signaling. All fibrosis data is from liver/cardiac models; no keloid-specific studies exist.
